Why Dev Academy?

Have been looking at the Dev Academy website off and on for 6 months wanting to take the plunge and learn new skills. So, finally did. Hope to one day work with a team of developers and also do some contract work as well.

Take on the DBC / EDA Experience:

What's your intake?
Bootcamp experience is different from the "schooling" usually received. Taking on the journey is the approach of diving in and making a mess and learning by doing.

What are your impressions?
I like learning by doing. I've done the university process and learned from that experience basic tools, but once on the job I had to learn what wasn't taught to me that was specific to job requirements. It's like building a foundation of learning layer by layer over the years to come.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?
I'm hopeful that I can take on learning this learning style and continue with it after graduation.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so how?
My expectations were all over the place prior to starting. I think over the past weeks I've been getting a better picture and will hopefully continue to do so.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?
I'm excited, nervous, apprehensive, hopeful, thrilled to finally doing it instead of just wondering and looking at your website.
